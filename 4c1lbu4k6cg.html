Recommended tiered methodology

The table below lays out our recommended assessment approach, with explanations for each of the nine steps in the right column.
1. Formulate the problem and the purpose of the assessment
        Emphasize public participation
2. Define the baseline
3. Create Conceptual model of ecosystem (focus on elements and processes)
        Identify bio-ecological receptors and their relationships (Network map)
        Relate model to the concept of ecosystem health
                Bioindicators
4. Identify the present drivers and the resulting stressors
5. Ranking stressors based on their individual effect
        Stressor hierarchy
6. Cumulative impact mapping
        Determining the appropriate geographic and temporal scales of analysis
7. Describing the combined effect of the stressors and taking into account their potential interactions
        Antagonistic, additive, synergistic
8. Conceptual model of stressor and ecosystem interactions
9. Quantification
        Statistical or mechanistic modelling and analysis
        Various options:    
  • Novel probabilistic ERA framework \citep{Goussen2016}
  • Novel regression approach, using non-linear parametric models \citep{Welle2017}
  • Adverse Outcome Pathways / AOPs (Mechanistic toxicology) \citep{Hooper2013}
  • Characterizing driver-response relationships in marine pelagic ecosystems for improved ocean management \citep{Hunsicker_2016}
  • Ecological modelling \citep{Fulton_2011}
  • Relative Risk Model 
The step are ordered according to the most logical order in relation to the information required in each step. As previously stated, our set of steps has been developed as a synthesis of four selected recommended methodological processes in the literature, which had already established series of steps (see appendix x). In appendix x, we highlight reoccurring steps with the same colors, and these same colors are used to highlight how our recommendations align with or differ from the reviewed tiered approaches, both in terms of included steps and their order. We've grouped the nine steps into four sections or stages: preparation, the stressor and ecosystem inventory, cumulative stressor interactions and finally the integration and quantification of the information aquired through the preceding steps (Fig. \ref{466921}).
